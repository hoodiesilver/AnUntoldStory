Refugee/Immigration

What is a refugee?

A refugee is someone who is outside of country or nationality and cannot return due to violence from conflicts or fear of persecution. Apportamately, 65 million people are displaced and over half of them are children.


What is an immigrant?

An immigrant is a person who has come across the border into a foreign country in order to live there.


What is an asylum sekker?

An asylum seeker is the title given to those that applied for asylum and are waiting to be refugees.


What is a prisoner of conscience?

A prisoner of conscience is someone who has not used or advocated violence but is imprisoned because of what they are or what they believe.


What is the difference between these titles?

The terms “refugee”, “asylum-seeker” and “migrant” are used to describe people who are on a mission, and have left their countries and have crossed borders to accomplish their goal. Even though they came from afar, they are still entitled to have all their human rights protected and respected, regardless of the status they have in the country they moved to.

  4. Referral for resettlement in the United States.

  5. Interview with State Department contractors.

  6. First background check.

  7. Higher-level background check for some.

  8. Another background check.
